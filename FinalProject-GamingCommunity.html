The Importance of the Gaming Community

Ethan Rangel

(Created 5/4/22)

People who play massively multiplayer online games, for example, report a stronger sense of social identity, higher levels of self-esteem and decreased feelings of loneliness. These games often encourage or require players to work together towards a goal, which can foster a sense of community and camaraderie. A healthy and engaged community can help boost the credibilty in the game that you play! If you show that you are able to have a dedicated fan base, you have the ability to attract the interest of the crucial sections of the gaming economy! Online gaming significantly aids in the development of many social skills that are vital to our well-being. It encourages both teamwork and communication skills. Online gaming also teaches leadership and quick decision-making skills, as well as patience and the ability to deal with sudden and unexpected situations.
